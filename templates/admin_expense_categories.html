Understanding Category Types
Operating Expenses

Day-to-day business costs not directly tied to production. Examples: Rent, salaries, utilities, marketing, office supplies, insurance, maintenance. These reduce your Net Profit.

COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)

Direct costs of producing goods or services sold. Examples: Raw materials, manufacturing costs, packaging, direct labor. These reduce your Gross Profit.

How This Affects Profit Calculation:
1
Revenue - COGS = Gross Profit
2
Gross Profit - Operating Expenses = Net Profit
